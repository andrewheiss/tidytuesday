    About Lemurs and the Duke Lemur Center

    The Duke Lemur Center houses over 200 lemurs across 14 species â€“ the most diverse population of lemurs on Earth, outside their native Madagascar.

    Lemurs are the most threatened group of mammals on the planet, and 95% of lemur species are at risk of extinction. Our mission is to learn everything we can about lemurs â€“ because the more we learn, the better we can work to save them from extinction. They are endemic only to Madagascar, so itâ€™s essentially a one-shot deal: once lemurs are gone from Madagascar, they are gone from the wild.

    By studying the variables that most affect their health, reproduction, and social dynamics, the Duke Lemur Center learns how to most effectively focus their conservation efforts. And the more we learn about lemurs, the better we can educate the public around the world about just how amazing these animals are, why they need to be protected, and how each and every one of us can make a difference in their survival.
